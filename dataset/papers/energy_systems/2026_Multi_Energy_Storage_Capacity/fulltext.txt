                                                           Incorporating the ammonia cycle process can further enhance system operation coordination. This paper es­
                                                           tablishes a bi-level collaborative optimization model for energy storage capacity configuration in an electricity-
                                                           heat-hydrogen-ammonia IES, accounting for the ammonia cycle process. Four energy storage forms (electricity,
                                                           heat, hydrogen, and ammonia) are integrated into the IES framework. First, an IES architecture incorporating
                                                           electricity-heat-hydrogen-ammonia storage is proposed. A multi-hydrogen coordinated ammonia production
                                                           method is developed, synthesizing hydrogen from renewable energy-based water electrolysis, natural gas
                                                           reforming, and fossil fuel-based processes. A hydrogen-to-ammonia conversion model is constructed, considering
                                                           the storage of electricity, heat, hydrogen, and ammonia. Second, a bi-level optimization model for multi-energy
                                                           storage configuration in the IES is formulated. The upper-level capacity configuration optimization model aims
                                                           to minimize the annual operation and maintenance costs of multi-energy storage devices and the system’s annual
                                                           dispatch cost. The lower-level low-carbon dispatch optimization model minimizes the system’s typical daily low-
                                                           carbon dispatch cost. The optimal capacity configuration and low-carbon dispatch scheme are derived through
                                                           iterative solving between the two levels. Furthermore, the ammonia co-firing ratio in coal-fired units is flexibly
                                                           constrained to regulate ammonia consumption. Finally, case studies demonstrate that the proposed model re­
                                                           duces both economic costs and carbon emissions in the system.

3. A framework for coordinated ammonia operation with multi-
hydrogen energy in the ammonia cycle

    The IES incorporates multi-hydrogen production pathways,
including renewable energy based green hydrogen, natural gas derived
G2H, and fossil fuel based F2H. These multi-hydrogen sources are co­
ordinated to synthesize ammonia, thereby meeting the system’s
ammonia demand (Detailed model in Appendix C). When the IES em­
ploys multi-hydrogen coordinated ammonia production, it prioritizes
green ammonia synthesis via electrolytic hydrogen and nitrogen ob­
tained through pressure swing adsorption. Should electrolytically pro­
duce hydrogen fail to satisfy the ammonia synthesis requirements of the
system, the system supplements with blue and gray ammonia, synthe­
sized respectively from natural gas derived hydrogen and fossil fuel-
based hydrogen, combined with PSA nitrogen. The ammonia produced                               Fig. 5. Load forecasts for different typical days.
in the synthesis plant is utilized for co-firing in thermal power units to
generate electricity, while surplus ammonia energy is stored in dedi­
cated ammonia storage systems [37]. The ammonia synthesis process
under this multi-hydrogen coordination framework is illustrated in
Fig. 2.

4. Coordinated bi-level synergistic optimization strategy for
ammonia production and multiple energy storage with multi-
hydrogen energy

    Given the intrinsic coupling between the multi-hydrogen coordi­
nated ammonia synthesis process and the electricity-heat-hydrogen-
ammonia energy storage system, while accounting for the temporal
disparity between energy storage capacity planning and low-carbon
operational scheduling, it is imperative to conduct a multi-timescale
analysis to evaluate the synergistic effects of annual storage capacity
allocation and daily low-carbon operation [38]. Accordingly, this study
develops a bi-level collaborative optimization model for integrated en­
ergy system planning, incorporating the ammonia cycle within an
electricity-heat-hydrogen-ammonia storage framework. The proposed
