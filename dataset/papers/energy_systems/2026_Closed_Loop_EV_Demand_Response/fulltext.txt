A R T I C L E I N F O                                   A B S T R A C T

Keywords:                                               With the increasing penetration of renewable energy sources (RES) in distribution network, the coordination of
Renewable energy source                                 demand-side electric vehicle (EV) becomes crucial for RES accommodation. By introducing the concept of
Electric vehicle                                        customer directrix load, this paper proposes a novel closed-loop corrective strategy to enhance the effectiveness
Demand response
                                                        of EV demand response. Firstly, a robust composite customer directrix load (CCDL) model is constructed to
Customer directrix load
Deviation correction
                                                        enhance resilience against uncertainties in EV responses, which integrates control characteristics and response
                                                        fluctuations to provide tailored full-time guidance for heterogeneous EVs. On the basis, considering that the
                                                        implementation of demand response is a sequential coupled process, a closed-loop correction mechanism is
                                                        further proposed to facilitate the multi-stage coordination across RES prediction, CCDL optimization, and de­
                                                        viation feedback. By developing a direction-aware combined prediction model to cope with asymmetric pre­
                                                        diction effects, and designing a policy-based gradient descent method for closed-loop refinement, the proposed
                                                        mechanism enables self-correction against uncertain deviations for demand response implementation. Numerical
                                                        comparison using real-world case demonstrates that the constructed CCDL model could effectively promote the
                                                        EV-coordinated RES accommodation, while the proposed deviation correction mechanism can improve the de­
                                                        mand response accuracy by 17.88 % over traditional sequential mode.

B. Data preprocessing and training configurations

    The data preprocessing mainly consists of several sequential steps. First, for each of the 12 photovoltaic stations, the raw hourly generation data is
organized into 24-h sequences, forming daily profiles with a shape of 24 × 12. All features are normalized to the range [0,1] using min-max scaling
based on the data set. Second, to support the training of policy learning network, the CDLs are optimized based on the proposed CCDL model using
historical photovoltaic data as input. Structured as daily profiles and temporally synchronized with the PV generation data, the CDLs are used as
supervision labels in the policy learning process. Third, supervised learning samples are constructed using a non-overlapping sliding window, which
are further split into training, validation, and test sets with a chronological split (70 %/20 %/10 %). Each sample includes 24 h of input features
(historical PV generation) and the subsequent 24 h of output targets (future PV generation or CDLs).
    For training configurations, all models are trained using the Adam optimizer with the parameter settings provided in Table A1. The training of sub-
prediction models is conducted for 50 epochs to achieve convergence under loss functions that emphasize surpluses and deficits. The learning rate
decay is applied with a factor of 0.5 every 10 epochs. After the sub-prediction models are trained, the policy learning network is trained separately
based on the proposed policy gradient method, with the loss defined as the decision deviation between the obtained and ideal CDLs, while the pa­
rameters of the sub-prediction models kept fixed.
